# Content Strategy — Love Over Exile

> What we publish, why, and how often.
> Last updated: 2026-03-01

---

## Strategic Position

Love Over Exile owns the **recovery and healing** space in parental alienation content. Competitors (PASG, law firms, clinical researchers) own diagnosis, legal, and academic content. LOE owns: *"What do I do now, and how do I hold on to myself while I do it?"*

The tone is always **resilience, not victimhood**. The language is warm, direct, and grounded in lived experience — never clinical or legalistic.

---

## Five Content Pillars

Every piece of content falls into one of these five pillars. Together they cover the full journey of an alienated parent.

### Pillar 1 — Understanding Parental Alienation

**What it covers:** Education, validation, naming the experience. The "is this what's happening to me?" content.

**Maps to site pages:** `/understanding`, `/faq`, articles

**Key topics:**
- What is parental alienation (definition, WHO classification, prevalence)
- Signs and warning signs of parental alienation
- Parental alienation vs estrangement — how to tell the difference
- The 17 tactics of alienation (Baker's framework)
- False allegations — the nuclear option
- The system that enables it (courts, social services, schools)

**Target keywords:** "what is parental alienation", "signs of parental alienation", "parental alienation tactics", "parental alienation vs estrangement"

---

### Pillar 2 — Coping & Survival

**What it covers:** Practical tools, communication strategies, day-to-day survival. The "what do I do?" content.

**Maps to site pages:** `/survival-guide`, articles

**Key topics:**
- How to cope as an alienated parent
- Communication with your co-parent (BIFF Method, Yellow Rock)
- Communication with your alienated child (Breadcrumb strategy)
- What NOT to do — the Logic Trap, Overcompensation, Guilt weaponization
- Building resilience (Stockdale Paradox, Sphere of Influence)
- Journaling and self-care practices
- A new way of loving (Agape vs Storge)

**Target keywords:** "how to cope as alienated parent", "parental alienation communication strategies", "alienated parent survival guide", "targeted parent strategies"

---

### Pillar 3 — Legal & Rights

**What it covers:** Legal landscape, court recognition, what alienated parents can and cannot do within the system. LOE does not give legal advice — it gives context and direction.

**Maps to site pages:** Articles, `/resources`

**Key topics:**
- Is parental alienation recognised by courts (jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction)
- What to document and how (for court)
- Working with family court professionals
- Legal options and their realistic outcomes
- The problem with the family court system (from a parent's perspective)

**Target keywords:** "parental alienation legal options", "is parental alienation recognised in court", "parental alienation evidence", "family court parental alienation"

**Note:** Always include a disclaimer that LOE does not provide legal advice. Direct readers to qualified family law professionals.

---

### Pillar 4 — Healing & Recovery

**What it covers:** The transformation beyond survival. Acceptance, forgiveness, meaning-making. This is LOE's strongest differentiator — almost no competitor covers this space.

**Maps to site pages:** `/inner-freedom`, articles

**Key topics:**
- Healing from parental alienation — is it possible?
- Radical acceptance (Linehan/DBT framework)
- The purpose of suffering (Frankl's three paths to meaning)
- Unconditional love as a decision, not a feeling
- The power of forgiveness (fourfold path)
- Rebuilding a relationship with an alienated child
- Adult children and parental alienation — healing decades later
- Daily practices for the dark hours

**Target keywords:** "healing from parental alienation", "parental alienation recovery", "rebuilding relationship with alienated child", "parental alienation mental health"

---

### Pillar 5 — The Book

**What it covers:** Love Over Exile itself — the story, the framework, why it exists, and how to get it.

**Maps to site pages:** `/the-book`, `/about`, articles

**Key topics:**
- Why Malcolm wrote Love Over Exile
- What the book covers (3-part structure)
- Excerpts and previews
- Launch updates and announcements
- Behind the scenes — the writing process
- Reviews and endorsements (post-launch)

**Target keywords:** "love over exile book", "parental alienation book", "Malcolm Smith parental alienation", "best books on parental alienation"

## Publishing Schedule

**Target cadence:** 2 articles per week

| Day | What |
|-----|------|
| Monday | Article 1 — typically Pillar 1, 2, or 3 (educational/practical) |
| Thursday | Article 2 — typically Pillar 4 or 5 (healing/book) |

**Why 2 per week:**
- Builds SEO authority steadily (100+ articles in the first year)
- Provides regular content for newsletter and social media
- Manageable pace for a one-person operation with AI assistance
- Each article gets proper attention — no rush, no filler

---

## Article Standards

| Attribute | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| **Length** | 1,200+ words minimum. 1,500–2,000 words ideal for SEO pillar articles. |
| **Tone** | Warm, direct, resilient. First person where appropriate. Never clinical. |
| **Structure** | H1 title → intro (hook + keyword) → 3-5 H2 sections → FAQ section → CTA |
| **SEO** | Primary keyword in title, H1, first 100 words, URL slug. Meta description under 155 characters. |
| **Internal links** | Minimum 2 per article: one to `/the-book` (waitlist), one to a related page or article. |
| **Images** | At least 1 per article. SEO filename, descriptive alt text. See `docs/seo-strategy.md` for naming convention. |
| **CTA** | Every article ends with a call to action — waitlist signup, free guide download, or community join. |
| **FAQ section** | 2–3 questions at the bottom of every article. Targets Google Featured Snippets. |

## Content Sources

### 1. Malcolm's Book Manuscript
The primary source. A 50KB summary is available in `content/source/`. The full manuscript covers parental alienation from personal narrative, research, practical strategies, and philosophical transformation. Every article should trace back to a concept from the book.

### 2. Original Research
Statistics, studies, and data on parental alienation. Key sources include:
- Harman et al. (prevalence studies — 22 million+ Americans affected)
- Baker (17 tactics taxonomy)
- Gardner (8 manifestations)
- Boss (Ambiguous Loss theory)
- Frankl (logotherapy and meaning)
- WHO ICD-11 classification

### 3. Community Stories
Once the community forum is established, real stories from alienated parents (anonymised, with permission) become powerful content. These are the most shareable and emotionally resonant articles.

## Content Repurposing

Every article is structured to be repurposed across multiple channels:

| Channel | What gets extracted |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Newsletter** | Summary paragraph (first 2-3 sentences of the article) + link |
| **LinkedIn** | Key quote or insight as a standalone post |
| **Instagram** | Key quote as an image with branded template |
| **LinkedIn Carousel** | 5-point list from the article as slides |
| **Twitter/X Thread** | 5-point list as a thread |

This repurposing is designed into the article template — not done as an afterthought.
