## What this book is about

If your child has been turned against you — through a high-conflict divorce, false allegations, religious pressure, or years of systematic manipulation — you already know that no one fully understands what this does to a person.

Not lawyers. Not most therapists. Not friends who tell you to "stay positive" or "give it time."

*Love Over Exile* was written from inside that pain. It holds two things at once that most books on this subject separate:

**Understanding the outside world:** What parental alienation actually is, how the system fails you, what tactics are being used against you, and what you can realistically do.

**Finding your way inside:** How the most devastating loss a parent can experience can become — if you are willing — the doorway to a depth of love and freedom you could not have reached any other way.

This is not a book about winning the legal battle. It is a book about surviving, and ultimately transcending, the worst thing that can happen to a parent.

---

## This book is for you if…

- Your child has rejected you, refuses contact, or has been turned against you
- You have faced false allegations — and the world treated you as guilty before any evidence was examined
- You have fought the courts, the therapists, and the system — and felt utterly alone in that fight
- You are still in contact with your child but feel the relationship being slowly eroded
- You have lost the legal battle and don't know how to survive what comes next
- You are years into this and still carrying wounds that haven't healed

You do not need to be a father. You do not need to be religious. You do not need to be in a particular country or legal system. If you have been exiled from your child's life, this book was written for you.

## Three parts. One journey.

### Part One — My Story

*Chapters 1–11*

Malcolm grew up in a closed Christian sect in the Netherlands, married within it, had three children, and left after his marriage ended. What followed was ten years of systematic alienation: a campaign of manipulation that included three separate false allegations of sexual abuse — each resulting in arrest, investigation, and the severing of all contact with his children.

Part One is a memoir of that decade. It is also, beneath the trauma, a story about a man being broken all the way open — and discovering, in that breaking, something he could not have found any other way.

---

### Part Two — Understanding Parental Alienation

*Chapters 12–20*

The most comprehensive and practical section. Drawing on peer-reviewed research, clinical frameworks, and hard-won personal experience, Part Two maps everything you need to understand and survive what is happening to you.

This includes:

- What the data actually shows (22 million+ affected parents in the US and Canada alone)
- How parental alienation meets the legal definition of domestic violence and coercive control
- The six players in the alienation system — and the role each one plays
- The 17 documented tactics used by alienating parents
- How false allegations work — and why the system is designed to treat you as guilty
- The specific psychological trauma experienced by targeted parents — including the 2D Alienation Trauma Pain Model
- A complete survival framework: what to do, what not to do, how to stay sane for the long haul
- How to communicate with an alienating co-parent without making things worse
- When — and how — to let go

---

### Part Three — Beyond Survival: Finding Inner Freedom

The final section is about what no survival guide can give you: the deeper transformation that only becomes possible when you stop fighting to get your old life back and allow yourself to be changed by what you have been through.

Drawing on Viktor Frankl, Marsha Linehan, Joseph Campbell, and the author's own breakthrough experience, Part Three offers a path through radical acceptance, unconditional love, and forgiveness — not as spiritual platitudes, but as living practices that have been tested in the most extreme conditions a parent can face.

## About Malcolm

Malcolm Smith is a Dutch-born author, father of three, and survivor of one of the most devastating experiences a parent can face: ten years of systematic parental alienation, including three false allegations of sexual abuse and the complete loss of contact with his children.

He did not write this book as a researcher observing from the outside. He wrote it from inside the fire — and from the other side of it.

His three children have since re-established contact with him. He lives in the Netherlands.

## Frequently asked questions

**When does the book publish?**
The book is in its final draft stage. A publication date has not yet been set. Join the waitlist to be the first to know.

**Is this book only for fathers?**
No. Parental alienation affects both mothers and fathers equally. The book is written for any parent who has been alienated from their child, regardless of gender.

**Does this book have a religious perspective?**
Malcolm's story is rooted in his experience growing up in a Christian sect, and his spiritual journey is central to Part Three. However, the book does not require or promote any specific religious belief. The frameworks in Part Three draw from secular psychology (Viktor Frankl, Marsha Linehan), human development theory (Joseph Campbell), and Malcolm's own direct experience. Readers of all beliefs — and none — have found this material meaningful.

**What makes this different from other parental alienation books?**
Most books on this subject are either clinical guides or personal memoirs. *Love Over Exile* is both — and then goes further. Part Two provides one of the most comprehensive practical frameworks available. Part Three addresses the question that survival guides cannot answer: what do you do when the battle is over and the wounds remain?
